Core Concepts

Simple Version

Carbon Dioxide + Water + Sunlight = Glucose + Oxygen

Chemical Formula

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

💡 Mr. Chloro Says: Six carbon dioxide molecules and six water molecules, with a sprinkle of sunlight, create one glucose molecule and six oxygen molecules. Sunlight in, sugar out!

Light Reactions

Location
Thylakoid membranes in chloroplasts
What Happens
Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight energy
Energy Conversion
Light energy becomes ATP and NADPH
Bonus Product
Oxygen released as waste

Dark Reactions

Also Called
Calvin Cycle
Location
Stroma of chloroplasts
What Happens
CO₂ converted into glucose
Energy Used
ATP and NADPH from light reactions

What It Is

A green pigment molecule in plant cells

Where It Lives

Inside chloroplasts, specifically in thylakoid membranes

What It Does

Absorbs red and blue light, reflects green light (that is why plants look green)

Why It Matters

Without chlorophyll, plants cannot capture light energy

☀️ Light Intensity

More light equals faster photosynthesis, up to a saturation point

💨 Carbon Dioxide

More CO₂ generally increases photosynthesis rate

🌡️ Temperature

Works best at 25-35°C for most plants

💧 Water

Essential raw material for the process

Interactive Activities

🧪

Activity 1: The Photosynthesis Lab

Materials Needed

Aquatic plant (elodea) Water Beaker Bright light Baking soda

Procedure

  1. Place elodea in water with dissolved baking soda
  2. Expose to bright light and observe oxygen bubbles
  3. Count bubbles per minute under different light intensities
  4. Graph results to see light intensity effect
🎨

Activity 2: Chromatography Color Hunt

Materials Needed

Leaves Rubbing alcohol Coffee filter paper Jar

Procedure

  1. Crush leaves and soak in alcohol to extract pigments
  2. Place filter paper strip in the solution
  3. Watch as different pigments separate by color
  4. Identify chlorophyll a, chlorophyll b, and carotenoids
🔬

Activity 3: Stomata Safari

Materials Needed

Microscope Leaf sample Clear nail polish Clear tape

Procedure

  1. Paint nail polish on leaf underside and let dry
  2. Peel off to create stomata impression
  3. Place on slide and observe under microscope
  4. Count stomata and observe guard cells
